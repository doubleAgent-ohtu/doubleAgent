### 4. Purpose and legal basis for processing personal data

#### Purpose of processing

Personal data stored in the user register is used for **authenticating the identity** of users (login) and **managing access rights** to ensure that the service is used only by University of Helsinki employees.

#### Legal basis for processing

The processing of personal data is based on Article 6(1) of the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR):

- **point (e)** (performance of a task carried out in the public interest), as the application relates to the performance of the University's statutory duties, and
- **point (f)** (legitimate interest of the controller), which in this case is the provision of an internal work tool for the University and ensuring its information security.

### 7. Retention period of personal data

Personal data is retained only as long as necessary for the purpose defined in section 4. Data is deleted when the person's right to use the application ends (e.g., upon termination of employment) or when the application is taken out of use.

### 8. Transfer of data outside the EU or EEA

Personal data is not disclosed to third parties.

Data is **not transferred** outside the EU or the EEA area.

---

### 9. Principles of register protection

The register is maintained as a technical record. Data traffic between the user's browser and the server is **encrypted (SSL/TLS)**.

The system and the data contained therein are protected by appropriate **firewalls, passwords, and other technical measures**.

Access to the data stored in the system is restricted to named University of Helsinki employees responsible for maintaining the application, who are bound by a **confidentiality obligation**. Access to data is limited only to those persons who need the information to perform their duties.

---

### 10. Rights of the data subject

Under the General Data Protection Regulation, the data subject has the right to:

- access their personal data
- request rectification of their personal data
- request erasure of their personal data ("right to be forgotten")
- request restriction of processing
- object to the processing of their personal data (where processing is based on legitimate interest)

Requests concerning the exercise of rights should be addressed to the contact person mentioned in section 3 or the Data Protection Officer of the University.

---
